Right now.

What I'm focused on this month. Not a status update — the actual centre of gravity. Reads as honest, not as a brag.

Day 1 of a 31-day sprint. Apr 30 → May 30. The first time I have committed to making the work in public on a clock. The point is not the prize at the end of the clock. The point is the version of me that exists after thirty days of refusing to skip.

The actual focus this month

Three main posts a day. Discount Trek joins from Day 5 — cardboard badges and phasers, no consoles, no soundstage, just a desk and a refusal to wait for permission. Cannes selection lands on Day 11. Whatever it returns, the work continues to Day 31.

Underneath the posting: rebuilding the actor's life I stepped away from a few years ago. Came back at 23. Eight months in. Most of the rebuild is unsexy — sides, self-tapes, watching the playback without flinching. The sprint is the visible part.

What's not the focus

Not chasing followers. Not optimising for the algorithm in real-time. Not waiting for the version of me that has it all figured out before posting. Not making the day job the villain — it funds the runway and that is a fair trade until it isn't.

What I'm specifically not doing

  • Comparing day-1 numbers to anyone else's day-1 numbers
  • Treating the playback as a vote on whether I get to keep going
  • Writing scripts for the audience I haven't built yet
  • Pretending the comparison-spiral isn't a daily appointment I keep
