Tax Prep + Expense Log

Self-employment tax + write-off categories. Set aside 25–30% of every dollar. Don't get blindsided in April.

⚠ The single most important tax rule

As soon as ANY income hits — TT Shop deposit, UGC payment, brand deal — move 25% to a separate savings account. Same day. Don't think about it. That money is the IRS's, not yours.

Creator-tax horror stories all start with "I forgot to set aside." Don't be the person who learns this in April with $8K owed and no savings.

The numbers

Self-employment tax

15.3% of net self-employment earnings (Social Security + Medicare).

Half is technically "employer side" — you pay both because you're both. No employer to split it with.

Federal income tax

~10% effective rate at $30K income (2026 brackets, single filer, standard deduction).

Going up as income climbs. At $50K → ~12%. At $80K → ~16%.

State tax

Depends on state. Range 0% (TX, FL, NV...) to ~10%+ (CA, NY).

Add to your set-aside %. If your state has 5% income tax, set aside 30% gross instead of 25%.

Practical rule of thumb

Set aside 25–30% of every gross dollar. Income on $2,400/mo → $600–$720 to tax savings monthly. Income on $5K/mo → $1,250–$1,500.

Quarterly estimated payments due Jan 15, Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15.

Write-off categories (track receipts)

📷 Equipment + tech

  • Camera, lenses, tripod, lighting, microphones
  • Computer (% used for content), external drives
  • Phone (% used for content)
  • Editing software (Adobe, CapCut Pro, etc.)
  • Cloud storage, hosting, domain costs

🏠 Home office

  • Dedicated room: simplified $5/sq ft (max $1,500/yr) OR actual % of rent + utilities
  • Internet (% used for work)
  • Cell phone bill (% used for work)
  • Office supplies (cardboard, glitter — if for content)

🎨 Content production

  • Props, costumes, set materials (DT cardboard, paint, glitter)
  • Subscriptions used for research/inspiration (Paramount+, Netflix if for parody research)
  • Music licenses, stock footage
  • Course/training fees (acting class, content workshops)

💼 Business operations

  • UGC platform fees
  • Patreon's 10% cut
  • Printful / Etsy / Shopify costs
  • Banking fees on business account
  • Accountant / bookkeeper fees (deductible!)

🚗 Mileage

  • Drives to filming locations (not home → print shop)
  • Drives for prop shopping
  • 2026 IRS rate: ~$0.67/mile (check current)
  • Track via app (MileIQ, Stride) — date, miles, purpose

🍔 Meals (50% deductible)

  • Meals during business meetings (50%)
  • Meals while traveling for content (50%)
  • Cannes festival meals (if attended for business)
  • Save receipts with notes — "lunch with brand X, discussed Y"

When to talk to a pro

  • Month 6 (~$1,800/mo): talk to a CPA who knows creators. Cost: $200–$500 for a setup call. Worth it.
  • Month 8 (~$3K+/mo): they'll likely recommend forming an LLC + S-corp election. Saves on self-employment tax above ~$40K/yr profit.
  • Don't do it yourself past $30K/yr — too many missed deductions and self-employment optimization missed.

First-year freelancers often skip Q1/Q2 because income is small. That's usually fine — you can pay all at year-end if total tax owed is under $1K. But once you cross ~$3K/quarter, start paying quarterly to avoid underpayment penalties.
