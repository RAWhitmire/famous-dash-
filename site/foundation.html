The Audience

Sarah — the friend across the table

She is the woman every Script Shift video is being said TO. When in doubt, write to her, not to a generic "audience."

Demographic spine

  • Age: 28–35 primary, 26–40 wider band
  • Gender: woman
  • Geo: US-heavy, English-speaking
  • Job: "fine on paper" — corporate-adjacent, decent salary, exhausted by it
  • Relationship: varied (single, partnered, recently exited a long-term thing)
  • Neuro-status: probably ADHD/AuDHD, diagnosed or strongly suspects

Emotional spine

  • Functional but cracking. Holding it together with caffeine + tape.
  • Once had a creative thing — acting, writing, music, performing — shelved it years ago.
  • Quietly grieves the version of herself she didn't become.
  • Resentful of the "be grateful" framing — she IS grateful, AND she's still mad.
  • Not interested in healing language; interested in being seen accurately.
  • Watches creators while doing dishes / lunch break / dog walk.

What Sarah responds to

  • Specific naming of the thing she's afraid to say out loud
  • Comedy that respects her intelligence (no "girlbosss," no soft-girl pandering)
  • Vulnerability that shows agency, not helplessness
  • Visible work, visible artifacts (cardboard, scripts, screenshots)
  • "This is rude but accurate" delivery
  • The "I caught myself" / "this is embarrassing because" tone
  • Star Trek references that don't require Trek knowledge to land

What makes Sarah scroll past

  • "Hey besties" / "girlies" / pandering
  • Healing/somatic/nervous-system/manifestation language
  • Productivity-bro framing
  • Polished "I figured it out" energy from a finished place
  • Vague "you got this" affirmations
  • Trauma-disclosure-as-performance (sharing for sharing's sake)
  • Anything that smells like a coach trying to sell her a course

Visual Anchor System

Per the Personal Branding doc: a strong brand has signature visuals that audiences recognize in three frames. Voice alone isn't enough — the eye has to file you instantly.

Primary anchor

🥣 The sugar bowl

In frame in 80% of videos — corner of desk, edge of frame, near elbow. Not announced, not pointed at. Just there. Audience starts commenting on it. It becomes the meme.

Why it works: specific, casual, NOT a logo. Cohesive with the "chaotic but strategic" voice.

Secondary anchor

🛸 Cardboard ENT-D + glitter

Discount Trek aesthetic. Drawn 2D bridge backdrop, visible cardboard joins, real glitter for FX. Used 100% on @discounttrek, 30–40% on @rawhitmire as crossover.

Tertiary anchor

🌙 Late-night editing desk

Editing desk B-roll, 2:15 AM clock visible, sugar bowl in frame, laptop glow on face. The "still up, still working" texture.

Talking-head signature

  • Lighting: mid-light room (NOT ring-light-perfect glow). Slight shadow on the off-side of face. Real-room natural light when possible.
  • Background: visible chaos — cardboard scrap, prop in soft focus, sugar bowl, sticky notes. NOT a curated influencer wall.
  • Outfit: simple black tank or oversized cardigan as base. Hair down, real, mid-day. Not styled for the camera.
  • Energy: talking to a friend, not performing for a feed. Slight slouch is allowed. Eye contact direct.

The visual brand is "she didn't sterilize her room for this video." That's the texture audiences trust.

Retention Mechanics · 2026 Standards

Every video has these 6 structural beats · target ~45s

Length update from live data: ~45s outperforms ~30s on this account. The longer length needs a 6th beat (Rehook 3) to prevent the 18–22s drop-off that kills longer videos. Add Rehook 3 to every video over 30s.

  • 0:00–3s · HOOK — visual motion + curiosity gap + pattern interrupt all firing simultaneously. Deadpan one-liner, mid-action cold open, phone-screen close-up, "wait" cliffhanger, contrarian claim, on-screen text flip, OR age-frame self-reflection (H16, proven).
  • 3–8s · REHOOK 1 — face cut, voiceover, B-roll insert, OR text overlay change. Resets attention before the first natural drop-off.
  • 8–18s · REHOOK 2 — stakes raise, twist, escalation, or new visual. The 15-second checkpoint is the second-steepest drop-off.
  • 18–28s · REHOOK 3 (NEW) — second twist, specific receipt, counter-move, escalation. Without this beat, longer videos sag and lose viewers right before climax.
  • 28–40s · CLIMAX — the artifact, the receipt, the side-by-side, the punchline. The thing that pays off the hook's promise.
  • 40–45s · LOOP HOOK — cliffhanger, callback, ironic smile, "watch the next one," or rewatch-bait that loops back to frame 1. Often paired with in-video CTA in the same beat.

Target: 70% completion rate. At ~45s = ~31s avg watch needed. The 6-beat structure is engineered for that benchmark.

⭐ Live wins integrated into v1.2

  • H16 — Age-Frame Self-Reflection ("what did you want to be at age 9?") proven to drive organic comment engagement. Sarah-coded vulnerability at lower entry cost.
  • CTA01 — Conditional Casual ("follow if that hit"). Minimal, lowercase, conditional. Outperforms studio-polished CTAs because it sounds like she'd actually say it on the phone. Voice-perfect.
  • Comment-section-as-content pattern — when a video invites audience to share their own version, the comments BECOME the content. Algorithm rewards engagement-on-engagement.

How to copy what worked: use the "follow if [X] hit" conditional pattern liberally. Use age-frame questions for any retrospective video. Build CTAs that sound like she'd say them on the phone — never studio-formal.

Caption + First-Comment System (NEW LAYER)

Per the Personal Branding doc: branding lives across every touchpoint. Caption is a SECOND hook. First comment is the lead-magnet bridge. Hashtags are the discovery layer.

📲 Caption (the second hook)

Concise. Curiosity-driven. Doesn't overexplain the video. Teases the ending without spoiling. Voice-locked. 1–2 sentences max.

Voice-guide reference captions:

"I thought I needed discipline. Turns out I needed to stop forcing myself into a life I didn't choose."

"I stopped waiting to be cast and immediately became everyone on the ship."

"This is either personal development or a cry for help. Possibly both."

"The role you're exhausted from playing might not even be yours."

"I fear the cardboard spaceship is making several points."

💬 First comment (PASSENGER ask)

Pinned by you within 60 seconds of posting. Bridges the video to the lead magnet. NOT a sales pitch — a continuation of the moment.

Templates (rotate):

  • Direct: "DM me PASSENGER and I'll send you the worksheet that names the role you've been performing. 📩"
  • Soft: "If this video named something out loud for you — DM me PASSENGER. I'll send you the 5 questions I used to figure out which role wasn't mine."
  • Specific: "If you're stuck on question #3 — what did it cost you — DM me PASSENGER. The worksheet has the prompts that broke that one open for me."
  • Sketch-tied (DT): "DM me PASSENGER if you want the worksheet. Yes, even though I'm in a Starfleet uniform. Especially because I'm in a Starfleet uniform."

Emotional Structure for Content (Voice Guide V1.0)
  1. Name the pain. Specifically. Not "burnout" — "the kind of burnout that happens when you're functioning but spiritually held together with tape."
  2. Make the sharp observation. The thing nobody says out loud. The "this is rude but accurate" beat.
  3. Connect it to a bigger pattern. Not just "me," but "us." The shared script we've all been performing.
  4. Show what you're doing differently. Visible artifact — the cardboard, the spreadsheet, the screenshot, the date.
  5. End with agency, humor, or a gut-punch line. Never end on helplessness. Always end on either action OR a perfectly-placed joke.
Voice Compliance Checklist

Before any output ships — hook, script, caption, comment, hashtag — confirm every box. If any check fails → revise before shipping.

  • ☐ Sounds like Raquel could actually say this out loud
  • ☐ Has at least one moment of humor, sharpness, or specificity
  • ☐ Does NOT use red-flag clinical/coach language (healing, trauma, nervous system, manifesting, guru, coach...)
  • ☐ Names the pain without making her sound fragile
  • ☐ Connects to one of the 5 content pillars
  • ☐ Passes the "would she cringe at this?" test
  • ☐ Avoids beige wellness energy
  • ☐ Has bite or weirdness, not just polish
  • ☐ NEW: Has hook in 0–3s, rehook at ~5s + ~15s, climax around 25s, loop hook in final 2s
  • ☐ NEW: Caption teases without spoiling
  • ☐ NEW: First comment bridges to PASSENGER lead magnet (or skips it intentionally for hero/vulnerability posts)
  • ☐ NEW: Visual anchor present (sugar bowl in frame, cardboard backdrop, OR late-night-desk texture)
