Meal Plan

Three tiers · pick what fits. Bought food is the default. Cooking is OPTIONAL, batch-only, never the default.

The principle

You're a picky eater. Cooking is exhausting. The plan inverts the normal advice: bought food is the system, not a splurge. Cooking is opt-in. Repetition kills appetite — rotation matters more than scratch-cooking.

Tier 1 · Default — Buy It EVERY DAY

Prepared, deli, takeout, grocery hot bar, frozen-meal-you-actually-like. The default. Not a splurge — the system.

  • Weekday lunches: grab-and-go at the print shop or near it
  • Dinners: grocery rotisserie / deli prepared / trusted takeout in rotation
  • Breakfasts: minimal-effort grab items on the way out
  • Treat night (Fri/Sat): the nice meal you actually want

Tier 2 · Optional Batch-Cook SUN OR WED · IF YOU FEEL LIKE IT

ONE pot of ONE thing that covers 4–6 meals. No assembly. No "5 different lunches." If you're not in the mood, skip — Tier 1 covers you.

  • One single-pot or single-tray dish (~60–90 min)
  • Eat from it 4–6 times across the week
  • Freeze half if you can't face it again by mid-week
  • Skipping it is allowed and expected

Tier 3 · Zero-Effort Fallbacks LOW-ENERGY DAYS

Legitimate options for tired days. Not failure. Listed explicitly so they stop counting as "I gave up."

  • Cereal for dinner
  • Peanut butter sandwich / toast
  • Cheese + crackers + fruit
  • Frozen pizza
  • Whatever is in the fridge, no plan

Why this looks different from before

The prior plan asked for Sunday 2-hour prep, 5 rotating lunches, 2–3 dinner bases, breakfast prep. That's a from-scratch cook routine you wouldn't sustain. The new plan: bought food default · cooking optional · zero-effort always allowed.

Aunt-fatigue insight: repeated dinners caused food burnout. Translation — rotation matters more than scratch-cooking. Tier 1 rotation across 3–4 trusted prepared options beats one batch you eat 5 nights in a row.

Bought food is the system. Cooking is the bonus.
