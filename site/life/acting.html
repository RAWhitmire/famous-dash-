Acting Practice Restart

Gently · 1 hour weekly to start. The acting career is the long arc — without practice, the whole thesis cracks. Build over weeks, not days.

Sun 5–6 PM · 1 hour weekly

  • 15 min · One monologue work-through (pick one, stick with it for the month)
  • 15 min · One sides cold-read
  • 15 min · Voice / breath / warm-up
  • 15 min · Notes / what felt off / journal one line
First Sunday: just 30 min. Build to 1 hour over 3 weeks.

When sides arrive (reactive)

  • Self-tape day = whichever evening fits before deadline
  • Print shop day eves: tape after dinner, 2 takes, send
  • Wed/Sun: more thorough — full coverage, multiple takes
No fixed schedule. The body cooperates when sides come.

Why this matters (read on weeks you skipped)

The long arc

Acting career = Arc 2. Without practice, the brand thesis cracks. Every video you make says "I'm a creative" — auditioning makes it true.

Practice content travels

"Restart your thing" beats hit hard with the audience. The practice itself is content. Voiceover work-throughs film well. The struggle is relatable.

Even 30 min keeps the muscle

Cold-reading drops fast without reps. 30 min/wk is enough to hold the floor. Once you're booking, daily becomes natural.

"Big sad" about no practice → flip into "the restart is the post."

The audience is the leverage. Auditions are the use.
