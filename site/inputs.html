Library · sources · inputs

Inputs.

Where the work comes from. Books, podcasts, films, creators, ideas. Annotated with one line each on what they actually gave me.

Books

  • Big Magic · Elizabeth Gilbert

    The "ideas are looking for a host" frame. Permission to take obsessions seriously without explaining them.

  • The War of Art · Steven Pressfield

    "Resistance" as a named opponent. Useful even when over-quoted.

  • Show Your Work · Austin Kleon

    The "build in public" thesis before it became a marketing term. Permission to share the process.

  • Audition · Michael Shurtleff

    The actor's bible. Twelve guideposts for any scene. Re-read every six months.

  • Polina (Cannes selector)

    The note that became the spine of the sprint. See Polina arc.

  • Cabel Sasser's notes

    Personal-site reference. Single-column, serif, no clutter, written in the way you actually think.

  • Robin Sloan

    Personal-site reference 2. Newsletter / website hybrid that reads like a working notebook.

Watching

  • Star Trek: TNG

    Source material for Discount Trek. Re-watching with the ear for the deadpan optimism, not the lore.

  • Industry (HBO)

    For pacing of dialogue under pressure. The way actors hold a beat when the scene is mid-deal.

  • Fleabag

    Direct address as a structural choice. The fourth-wall break that lands as a character's most honest moment.
