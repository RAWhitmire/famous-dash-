If Things Break

Zero-tolerance posting means contingency plans matter. Ten things that can go wrong and exactly what to do.

⚠ The zero-tolerance rule

No skipped slots. Period. If a slot is going to miss, you pull from backup bank or you film a 30-sec emergency post on your phone. Nothing else.

The algorithm punishes inconsistency more than it rewards quality. A mediocre post beats no post. A ghost-day breaks the momentum compound.

10 things that can go wrong + the play

1. You're sick

Play: pull from backup bank for all 3 slots. Reduce DT to 1 post or skip if necessary. Rest. Refill bank within 48 hrs of recovery.

2. Wifi out at editing time

Play: CapCut works offline — finish edit. Upload via mobile data tether. If mobile data also dead → coffee shop wifi. If that fails → pull from backup bank, queue when wifi returns.

3. Phone dies during a shoot

Play: charge for 15 min, get to 30%. If shoot was for tomorrow's slots, you have time. If it was today's slot 3, pull from backup bank.

4. An idea bombs after filming

Play: don't post bad content. Pull from backup bank, file the bombed video as "lessons learned." Was the hook weak? The premise unclear? Note for next time.

5. Wednesday batch falls through

Play: Sunday becomes the emergency batch day (full 4-hour shoot, not just 90 min). Skip Tier 2 cooking, skip optional acting. Sleep schedule shifts: 11 PM hard cap.

6. DMCA strike on a DT post

Play: remove the post. Don't counter-notice. Note the trigger (which IP element). See IP strategy. Replace with a tweaked version pushing the parody framing harder.

7. A post goes viral and you can't keep up with comments

Play: reply to first 50 comments thoughtfully. Bulk-pin the most interesting ones. Make a follow-up video addressing themes. Schedule a Live for the next evening.

8. Print shop mandates extra hours

Play: light days become no-film days. Wednesday batch absorbs the hit (extend to 5 hr if needed). Backup bank covers gap days. Don't try to film exhausted after a long shift.

9. Mental health crash

Play: backup bank for 3+ days. Reduce DT to nothing if needed. Tell trusted person. The print-shop is your safety net — you're not abandoning the sprint, just pausing the heavy lift. Sleep is the biggest lever.

10. You have a great idea but no time to film it

Play: capture it in idea bank immediately. Schedule for next batch day. Don't try to squeeze it in — squeezed shoots produce mediocre content.

The 30-second emergency post

If everything fails simultaneously

Worst case: backup bank empty, energy gone, slot in 1 hour. Pick ONE:

  1. Direct address ramble — "Real talk, this whole sprint is harder than I thought, here's what I'm learning..." Vulnerability sells.
  2. "Day in the life" 1-shot — turn camera on, narrate what you're doing for 60 sec, post.
  3. Voiceover over apartment B-roll — film 30 sec of your space, voice it later, post.
  4. Reply to your top comment — pull the most interesting comment from the past week, react.

All 4 of these are <30 min total. None require setup. Use them.

The wheels stay on with backup + improv. The wheels come off only if you let them.
