The Get Ahead Plan

When you have downtime, what do you do to bank ahead for tomorrow? The plan that separates "drowning" from "comfortable" by Day 14. Stack buffer when energy allows; coast on the buffer when it doesn't.

The principle

Posting 3/day for 31 days only works if you BUFFER ahead. Filming the morning's post on the morning you post is how creators burn out. The goal: by Day 7, you have 3 days of buffer. By Day 14, you have 5.

When buffer drops below 2 days, that's a yellow flag. Below 1 day = red flag. Buffer is the difference between "creative pressure" and "panic pressure."

Three time-windows for getting ahead
⚡ Daily micro-leverage (15–30 min)

End-of-edit-block bonus

After tonight's slot 3 is queued, you have ~15–30 min before wind-down. Don't crash on the couch — invest 15 min into ONE of:

  • Outline TOMORROW's slot 1 hook
  • Film 1 voiceover for an upcoming process video
  • Pull 3 trending sounds + save them with notes
  • Photo dump around the room for a future carousel
  • Write 1 storytime hook into the idea bank

Compound effect: 15 min × 5 weekdays = 75 min/week of pre-built content for free.

📅 Weekly mid-leverage (1.5–2 hr)

Sunday afternoon banking

Sunday 3:30–5:00 PM is in the "light filming if needed" block. Use it strategically — film 3–5 short videos that DON'T require costume/setup:

  • Direct-address takes on multiple topics (record 5, edit later)
  • Voiceovers for B-roll you've already shot
  • Carousel content (photos + captions)
  • Reaction/duet content (low-prep)
  • Storytime monologues to camera

Sunday is your bank deposit. Wednesday is the big withdrawal. Make sure both happen.

🎬 Big-leverage (Wednesday batch)

The 14-video shoot

Wednesday 9–4 PM is the entire week's safety net. The framing isn't "film one day's content" — it's "film the week's content."

  • Block A (9–12): warm-ups + 4 medium-effort videos (Direct Address, Storytime)
  • Block B (1–4): 4–6 high-effort videos (DT character, Sketch, Process)
  • Block C (3:30–4): 2–4 quick-fire (Carousels, voiceovers, B-roll)

Target: 10–14 main + 4 DT in one shoot. That's 4 days of main slots covered + the DT track.

If Wednesday wobbles, the rest of the week wobbles. Protect this day like it's a paid gig.

What to film on a "no idea" day

When you have time but no inspiration, fall back to the "easy gets ahead" list:

  1. Direct Address ramble — pick a topic from the idea bank, talk for 60 sec, edit later
  2. Room B-roll — film 5 minutes of desk/cardboard/sticky-wall footage. Voiceovers find their home in editing.
  3. Carousel grab — take 8 photos of a ridiculous thing in the room. Captions write themselves later.
  4. Q&A bank — record 3 reply-to-comment style answers using fake comments you'd want to receive
  5. Trend exploration — open TT Discover, find 3 trending sounds, plan how DT/you would use each

All of these are sub-30-minute investments that produce 2–4 future-day deposits.

When NOT to get ahead

Recovery > buffer

If you're under 7 hr of sleep for 3+ nights, OR Tuesday rest fell through 2 weeks running — DO NOT use spare time to film more. The buffer compounds, but so does sleep debt, and sleep debt destroys the format-test phase.

When in doubt: rest > batch > daily-leverage. Trust the order.

Buffer is the difference between creative pressure and panic pressure. Bank when you can.
