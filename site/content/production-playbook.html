Production Playbook

Open this before every filming session. Hook formulas in voice. Caption templates. First-comment templates with PASSENGER ask. Hashtag stacks. Retention reminders. Compact and scannable on phone.
The 6 retention beats · target ~45s · every video, no exceptions
0–3sHOOK
VO + visual + on-screen text (stack all 3)
3–8sREHOOK 1
fresh hook type · face cut + new specific
8–18sREHOOK 2
fresh hook type · twist or stakes raise
18–28sREHOOK 3
fresh hook type · second twist or receipt
28–40sCLIMAX
fresh hook type · artifact or payoff lands
40–45sLOOP
fresh hook type · cliffhanger or callback

Length rule: ~45s target. Live data confirms 45s outperforms 30s for this account. Target: 70% completion = ~31s avg watch time.

⚡ Two non-negotiables

1. "Visual-only" hook ≠ silent. Every 0–3s window has VO/dialogue PLUS the visual cue PLUS on-screen text. The visual is an anchor that rides parallel to the voice — never a substitute for it. There is no scenario where the first 3 seconds of any video is silent.

2. Keep rehooking. Rehooking does not stop at beat 2. Every single beat after the hook is a fresh hook attempt — different hook type ideally — landing every 5–7 seconds. Rephrase, re-tease, raise stakes, drop a new specific receipt. Treat the 6-beat strip as 6 hooks in a row, not "1 hook + 5 body beats."

Why 6 beats, not 5?

At ~45s the algorithm is hunting for retention dropoff at 18–22s — that's where viewers who didn't get hooked twice would normally bail. Rehook 3 (the new beat at 18–28s) is a "second twist" or specific receipt that resets attention before climax. Without it, the middle of long videos sags. Add it on every video over 30s.

Hook stacking — types to layer per beat

Each of the 6 beats should fire as many of these as possible at the same time. Diversity wins; one beat firing 4 hook types beats four beats firing only one each.

  • Verbal hook — the spoken line itself (one of H01–H16)
  • Visual hook — motion, cut, costume reveal, prop, location shift, expression change
  • On-screen text hook — short overlay text that's NOT the same as the VO (adds a second curiosity gap)
  • Audio cue hook — sound effect, beat drop, vocal whisper, sigh, sudden silence after sound
  • Pattern interrupt — a beat the viewer doesn't see coming (zoom punch, jump cut, prop swap, fourth-wall break)

Rule of thumb: if a beat is just "she keeps talking" with no second hook type firing, that beat is dead — replace it.

Hook Formulas (in voice)

15 hook patterns Raquel can mix-and-match. Each tagged with which pillar it fits + 3 voice-locked examples. Rule: first 1.5 seconds = visual motion + specific claim + pattern interrupt all firing. NO "hey guys" warmups.

H01 · The Diagnostic P1 P4

"You're not [common label]. You're [accurate reframe]."
Examples:

"You're not lazy. You're trying to force yourself to stay in a life your body already rejected."

"You're not behind. You're auditioning for a role that wasn't written for you."

"You're not burned out from doing too much. You're burned out from performing a life you never chose."

H02 · The Casual Confession P1 P5

"Unfortunately, [casually-dropped dramatic truth]."
Examples:

"Unfortunately, my coping mechanism developed comedic timing."

"Unfortunately, I cannot perform discipline for a life that isn't mine."

"Unfortunately, I figured out the role I was cast in. So now I am quitting."

H03 · The Embarrassing Admission P1 P5

"This is embarrassing because [specific reason], but [admission]."
Examples:

"This is embarrassing because I'm 23 — but I just figured out I've been people-pleasing my own family since I had teeth."

"This is embarrassing because I literally went to drama school — but I was performing in real life and didn't notice."

"This is embarrassing because I am an actress — but I forgot I had wants of my own for, like, 8 years."

H04 · The Receipt P2 P3 P4

"[Number] [things] in [time]. Here's what changed."
Examples:

"31 days. 93 posts. Zero skipped. Here's what stopped feeling weird at video 3."

"4 hooks since Polina said mine was generic. Rate them."

"6 weeks of posting cardboard Star Trek. Here's what I learned the algorithm doesn't reward."

H05 · POV Trope (Sarah-coded) P1 P4

"POV: you [Sarah-specific scenario]"
Examples:

"POV: you're 31, technically functioning, and your body just told you no."

"POV: you said you'd start the creative thing 'when life calmed down' five years ago."

"POV: you're at the print shop wondering why you have 47 tabs open in your head."

H06 · The Caught-Myself P1 P4 P5

"I caught myself [doing the thing] today. [The implication]."
Examples:

"I caught myself apologizing for taking up space. In my own apartment. To my cat."

"I caught myself rewriting a customer's headline at the print shop. I'm Polina now. Unfortunately."

"I caught myself organizing snacks for a meeting that was rescheduled. The role is so deep I'm casting myself in it."

H07 · Storytime cold-open P1 P3 P5

"The most [unhinged/embarrassing/specific] thing happened [today/at the print shop/last night]."
Examples:

"The most unhinged thing happened today. I tried to film a Star Trek parody and the cardboard fell on me."

"The most embarrassing thing happened. I cried in the print shop bathroom because someone said my hook was generic."

"Storytime: how a single comment made me delete and recut a video at 1 AM."

H08 · Contrarian receipt P1 P2

"I did [the thing everyone says works]. [Counter-intuitive result]."
Examples:

"I applied a Cannes selector's note for 3 weeks. Views went UP. Engagement DROPPED."

"I tried to be discipline-coded for a year. Got more done. Felt worse. Stopped."

"I quit acting for my parents. Got a real job. Body refused. Full revolt at 2:15 AM."

H09 · Object Hook P3 P5

"[Specific object close-up + non-sequitur claim]."
Examples:

[Sugar bowl close-up] "I am building my dream life with cardboard, spite, and a suspicious amount of Star Trek knowledge."

[Phaser hot-glue close-up] "This is either a prop or a personal essay. Possibly both."

[Glitter falling close-up] "I fear the cardboard spaceship is making several points."

H10 · The Math Hook P1 P4

"The math is mathing: [specific equation]."
Examples:

"The math is mathing: 8 years of saying yes to roles I didn't audition for = 1 night of crying about cardboard."

"The math is mathing: 47 tabs open in my head + zero water consumed + 'why am I tired' is a complete formula."

"The math is mathing: I quit acting for $19/hour. The trade was bad. I am unquitting."

H11 · Direct Address Diagnosis P1 P4

"Some of you are not [problem]. You are [accurate reframe]."
Examples:

"Some of you are not lost. You are still following directions from people who never knew where you were going."

"Some of you are not unmotivated. You are exhausted from performing a life you didn't audition for."

"Some of you are not procrastinating. You are refusing to start something your body knows isn't yours."

H12 · The "Wait" Cold Open P1 P5

[Mid-sentence/mid-action close-up] "Wait." [Cut to context]
Examples:

[Mid-printer counter at print shop] "Wait. I just talked to a customer the way Polina talked to me. About a yoga flyer."

[Mid-laundry fold] "Wait. I'm folding my Starfleet uniform. Out of the dryer. Like that's a normal Wednesday."

[Mid-coffee pour] "Wait. Did I just describe my life as a 'role I'm finishing out'? In therapy I don't go to?"

H13 · The "I did not audition for this" P1 P2 (signature)

"I am no longer available for [role/script/dynamic]. I did not audition for this."
Examples:

"I am no longer available for the role of 'reasonable older sister.' I did not audition for this."

"I am no longer available for jobs that require me to flatten my voice. I did not audition for this."

"I am no longer available for relationships that need me to be 30% smaller. I did not audition for this."

H14 · The "Spiritually held together" P4 P5 (signature)

"There is a very specific kind of [thing] that happens when [Sarah-specific situation]."
Examples:

"There is a very specific kind of burnout that happens when you are technically functioning but spiritually held together with tape and a Celsius."

"There is a very specific kind of grief that happens when you realize you've been performing a version of yourself for 8 years."

"There is a very specific kind of rage that happens when you fold a uniform you bought for a job that didn't deserve you."

H15 · Object-as-Argument P3 P5

"This [object] is either [thing] or [thing]. Possibly both."
Examples:

"This cardboard ENT-D bridge is either personal development or a cry for help. Possibly both."

"This sugar bowl is either a snack or a personality. The math says yes."

"This Starfleet uniform is either a costume or a confession. I refuse to clarify."

H16 · Age-Frame Self-Reflection ⭐ P1 P2 (proven)

"What did you want to be at age [6/9/12]? Have you done it?"
Why this works (live data):

⭐ PROVEN. The age-9 video is generating organic comments from viewers sharing their own answers + whether they followed through. Comment section becomes the content. Forces a mental pause at second 3 (audience answers in their head before continuing) = retention spike. Self-segments the audience: Sarahs stay because they haven't done it; people who did stay because they're proud. Both engage.

Examples:

"What did you want to be at 9? Have you done it? I'll go first — actress. I quit at 23. I un-quit last month."

"At age 6 you knew. At age 12 you started doubting. By 18 you'd traded it for 'sense.' What was yours?"

"Tell me what your 9-year-old self called her dream. Tell me if you've kept your word to her."

"What was your dream at age 9 that you swapped for 'a real job'? Mine wanted to perform. I named the swap last week."

SERIES POTENTIAL · don't burn it on one video:

· "Age 6 vs age 9 vs age 12 — when did you swap?"
· "What did your dream cost when you swapped?"
· "POV: your 9-year-old self watches you on a Tuesday"
· "Reply to the comments from the age-9 video"
· Carousel of audience answers from comment section
· Variant ages by audience cohort (age 6 for younger viewers, age 16 for late-bloomers)

CTA Patterns (in voice · in the video itself)

The line at the end of the video — separate from caption + comment. Voice-locked, low-pressure, NEVER "follow for more content!" energy. Live-data note: a minimal "follow if that hit" outperformed studio-quality CTAs because it sounds like the way she'd actually say it. Tone over polish.

CTA01 · Conditional Casual ⭐ (proven)

"[action] if [thing] hit"

"follow if that hit"

"save if you needed this"

"comment yours, I read every one"

"share if your 9-year-old self approves"

CTA02 · The Permission

"[action] if [specific scenario]. or don't. either is correct."

"save if you tried to quit yours. or don't. either is correct."

"follow if you're also still in the lab. no pressure. I'll be here."

"comment what your 9-year-old wanted. or don't say it out loud yet. I get it."

CTA03 · The Going-First

"I'll go first — [her answer]. Tell me yours."

"I wanted to be an actress. I'll go first. Tell me yours 👇"

"I quit. I un-quit. Comment your version."

"My role I didn't audition for: 'reasonable older sister.' What's yours?"

CTA04 · The Witness Invite

"[action] this for [the person you used to be]."

"save this for your 9-year-old self."

"send this to whoever needs it. that includes past you."

"pin this for the next time you forget."

CTA05 · The 47-Tabs (ND-coded)

"[ADHD-honest invite that's not productivity-coded]"

"I will be here in 47 tabs if you want to talk about it"

"comment now or in 6 months when you remember. either works."

"save this in the same place as the other things you mean to come back to. that drawer."

First-Comment Templates (PASSENGER bridge)

Pin within 60 seconds of posting. The bridge from the video moment to the lead magnet. Not a sales pitch — a continuation of the moment. Skip on hero/vulnerability posts where the ask would feel transactional.

FC01 · Direct (default)

"DM me PASSENGER for the worksheet that names the role you've been performing. 📩"
When to use:

Most Script Shift videos. Default unless something else fits better.

FC02 · Soft tie-in

"If this video named something out loud — DM me PASSENGER. I'll send you the 5 questions I used to figure out which role wasn't mine."
When to use:

Vulnerable posts where "send me the worksheet" would feel pushy. The "if this hit" framing makes it feel earned.

FC03 · Specific question hook

"If you're stuck on [specific question from worksheet] — DM me PASSENGER. The worksheet has the prompts that broke that one open for me."
When to use:

When the video naturally led to one of the 5 reframe questions. E.g., "What did it cost you?" video → comment about cost question.

FC04 · DT crossover

"DM me PASSENGER if you want the worksheet. Yes, even though I'm in a Starfleet uniform. Especially because I'm in a Starfleet uniform."
When to use:

DT crossover posts on @rawhitmire. Self-aware tone bridges the parody back to the brand.

FC05 · Skip the ask

[No first comment OR a non-sales comment that responds to the moment]
When to use:

Hero vulnerability posts (e.g., "Polina, if you're watching"). Posts that close arcs. Tone is everything — when the ask would cheapen the moment, skip it. Quality of the moment > a few extra DMs.

Case Study #1 · The Age-9 Video

What happened: Posted a video asking what the audience wanted to be at age 9. CTA was "follow if that hit lmao." Comments organically filled with viewers sharing their own age-9 dreams + whether they've done it or not.

Why it worked:

  1. Universal hook — every viewer has an answer to "what did you want to be at 9?" Forces mental engagement at second 3 = retention spike.
  2. Self-segments the audience perfectly — Sarahs (haven't done it) feel seen and stay; people who DID it feel proud and stay. Both groups engage.
  3. Lower-stakes vulnerability — easier to share "I wanted to be a vet" than "I'm performing a life I didn't choose." Same content, less guarded entry.
  4. Comment section becomes the content — every reply is a mini-story. Other viewers stay to read replies. Algorithm reads engagement-on-engagement as a strong signal.
  5. Voice-perfect CTA — "follow if that hit" is exactly how Raquel would say it out loud. Lowercase, conditional, low-pressure. NOT "follow for more content!" energy.

Pattern extracted: H16 (Age-Frame Self-Reflection) + CTA01 (Self-Aware Casual). Don't burn this on one video — it's an entire series potential spanning ages 6, 9, 12, 16, 18 + reply-to-comments + "swap moment" + audience-reply carousels.

What to copy from this win: the "follow if [X] hit" conditional CTA pattern across other videos · the "what did you want at age N + have you done it" frame for any retrospective post · CTAs that sound like the way she'd actually say it on the phone, NOT studio-polished asks.

Pre-Filming Checklist (quick scan)
  • ☐ Length target ~45s (data-confirmed sweet spot for this account)
  • ☐ Sugar bowl in frame (or other primary visual anchor)
  • ☐ Hook is one of H01–H16 AND verbal + visual + on-screen text are ALL stacked in 0–3s (no silent cold opens — VO runs from the first frame)
  • ☐ Rehook 1 at ~5s = a different hook type than the opener (if H01 was verbal, R1 should add object/visual; if opener was visual, R1 stacks pattern interrupt + new specific)
  • ☐ Rehook 2 at ~15s = another fresh hook type (twist, stakes, escalation, audio cue, jump cut)
  • ☐ Rehook 3 at ~22s = another fresh hook type (second twist, specific receipt, counter-move, on-screen text reveal)
  • ☐ Climax artifact at ~30s = its own hook beat (the receipt, side-by-side, payoff visible) — NOT just "the body wraps up"
  • ☐ Loop hook in final 2–5s (cliffhanger, callback, ironic smile, sentence that re-leads to the opener)
  • ☐ Every beat fires ≥2 hook types simultaneously (verbal + visual / verbal + on-screen text / visual + audio cue, etc.) — no "she just keeps talking" beats
  • ☐ In-video CTA drafted (CTA01–CTA05, voice-locked, "follow if that hit" conditional-casual energy)
  • ☐ Caption drafted (C01–C05 template)
  • ☐ First comment drafted (FC01–FC05 template, OR skip if hero/vulnerability)
  • ☐ Hashtag stack picked (3 pillar + 2 evergreen, fresh combo)
  • ☐ Voice Compliance Checklist passed (see Foundation)
