Trend lifecycle

A trend has 3 phases: EMERGING (use 1K–100K, biggest payoff if you're early) → PEAK (100K–10M, safer but more crowded) → DECLINING (sound dropping in new uses, avoid).

From first appearance to dead = usually 7–14 days on TikTok. Faster on viral sounds, slower on niche ones.

The discipline: set an expiry date when you log a trend. After expiry, archive — don't post.

How to scout trends (15 min/day)

📍 Where to look

  • TT Discover → Sounds tab → "Trending"
  • TT Creative Center (creativecenter.tiktok.com) → free trend dashboard, sortable by region
  • For You feed while scrolling — note any sound used 3+ times in 1 session
  • Other creators in your niche — what are Trek/parody/comedy creators using right now?

✅ Use checklist

  • Velocity: sound used 5–10x more this week than last? Yes = use.
  • Audience match: does the OG context fit Trek/parody/DIY? Or can you bridge it?
  • Saturation: is your specific niche already flooded with it? If yes, skip — you're late.
  • Originality angle: can you do something with this nobody else has yet?
Format trends (not just sounds)

Sometimes the trend is a structural pattern, not a specific sound. Examples:

  • "Tell me you're X without telling me you're X" — evergreen format, cycles in/out
  • POV transitions with text overlays
  • Day-in-the-life with timestamps as on-screen text
  • "I'm normal" with chaotic visual contradicting
  • Long pause then punchline with text reveal

Format trends move slower than sound trends — usually 30–60 day lifecycles. Worth investing more energy into adapting.
