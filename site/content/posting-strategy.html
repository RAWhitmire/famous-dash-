Slot mechanics · what fires at 9am vs 1pm vs 5pm

Each slot has a different audience state, a different mood, a different optimal hook type. Match content type to slot state — don't randomize.

9 AMscroll-pause

Slot 1 · 9:00 AM · "first impression"

Audience state: coffee scroll, commute, getting ready. Cold attention. Need the strongest pattern interrupt of the day. Algorithm reads first-of-day engagement as a signal that distributes the rest.

Best hook types: H01 Diagnostic ("you're not lazy, you're..."), H16 Age-Frame ("what did you want at age 9?"), H11 Direct Address Diagnosis. Universal scroll-stoppers.

Avoid here: hero vulnerability posts (too cold), DT character monologues (too niche for first impression), inside-baseball content.

Production reality: queued night before. Filmed during Wed batch or Sun light filming. Slot 1 NEVER films same-day.

1 PMlunch break

Slot 2 · 1:00 PM · "relatable pause"

Audience state: mid-day break, slightly less guarded. Already had 1-2 hours of work and is annoyed. Open to "this is rude but accurate" diagnostics. Best slot for comments-baiting.

Best hook types: H02 Casual Confession, H06 Caught-Myself, H12 "Wait" Cold Open, H08 Contrarian Receipt. Mid-day energy = punchier.

Best for: trend remixes (peak velocity at midday), reply-to-comment videos, audience-question hooks. Engagement-baiting posts go HERE because comments fire fastest at lunch.

Production reality: filmed previous evening or Wed batch. Sometimes can be timely (e.g. trend remix from a sound that just popped).

5 PMend-of-work

Slot 3 · 5:00 PM · "release valve"

Audience state: wrapping up work, leaving-the-office scroll. Tired but not yet evening-reflective. This is NOT the deep-vulnerability slot — it's the "end of my workday, give me something to laugh at or feel" slot.

Best hook types: H05 DT Character Monologue, H09 Object Hook, H15 Object-as-Argument, H07 Storytime cold-open. Slightly more chaotic energy works here.

Best for: DT crossovers, atmospheric/texture posts (2:15 AM editing desk), playful self-roast posts, sketch reveals. The "give them the dessert" slot.

Production reality: often films from Wed batch or Sun. Can also be cross-post timing (Slot 3 = best slot for the DT post if doing a single DT post that day).

Slot ordering ruleOne PILLAR 1 (Script Shift / identity) post per day minimum. The other two slots can vary across pillars. Don't load 3 vulnerability hero posts in one day — that drowns the audience.
The 11-day audience journey · Phase 1 of sprint

Each post serves a stage. The audience is moving through a narrative arc whether you plan it or not — better to plan it.

Audience is meeting Raquel for the first time. Goal: establish brand fingerprint in 9 posts. They should leave Day 3 able to fill in "she's the woman who ___."

Audience knows who. Now they want proof. Goal: show the lab. Show the Polina arc landing. Show the cardboard becoming sketches. Build credibility.

Hero post: M10 (T-5 spine — "real chat with myself") on Day 4. M14 (Polina note application) on Day 5.

DT phase shift: Day 5 = first full DT sketch drops (Salt Vampire). Audience has watched 4 days of build content — payoff lands.

What audience should walk away knowing: she's not just talking about it — she's actually shipping. Polina note arc is real. The lab works. The DT is funny.

Days 8–10 (May 7 – May 9)

Stage 3 · WHY does this matter?

Audience is invested. Stakes need to raise. Cannes day approaches. Goal: build emotional investment before the May 10 result.

Posts: M22 (T-3 family escalation), M25 (self-tape prep — long-arc reminder), M26 (need-it + do-it-again posture), M27 (Polina capstone), M28-30 (Cannes-eve build).

What audience should feel: invested in the May 10 outcome. Rooting for her. Wants to see what happens.

Day 11 (May 10) · CANNES

Stage 4 · The Cannes Day arc

Result day. Three posts ship regardless of outcome. Slot 1 = "I'm at the print shop, Wednesday looks like Tuesday either way" (already hinted in M17/M30). Slot 2 = result reaction (filmed reactive). Slot 3 = "33 in 11 days, the rewrite worked" closing the phase.

Critical: Slot 1 + Slot 3 are PRE-FILMED and ship from queue. Only Slot 2 is reactive. The whole "Tuesday looks like Tuesday" thesis depends on the audience seeing posts ship at 9am regardless of result.

Hero post cadence · max 2/wk, no exceptions

Hero posts are vulnerability-anchored, slow-paced, eye-contact-held videos. They cost the audience emotional energy. Spend them carefully. Max 2/week early in sprint, max 1/week as audience grows.

What counts as a hero post

  • T-1 spine (M07 — "couldn't quit acting")
  • T-5 spine (M10 — "real chat with myself")
  • T-7 Polina pulverization (M04)
  • "Polina if you're watching" (M92 close)
  • Wednesday Bridge post (M40 — Arc 1 → Arc 2)
  • Final Cannes/Prize day posts (M31-33, M91-93)

~6-7 hero posts over 31 days. Average 1.5/wk.

Hero post placement rules

  1. Always Slot 1 or Slot 3 — never lunch break (wrong energy)
  2. Never 2 days in a row — audience needs recovery
  3. Never paired with another hero in same day's other slots
  4. Day after a hero = lighter content (humor, process, DT)
  5. Skip the PASSENGER ask if it would cheapen the moment (use FC05)
  6. Pin the comment, but ONLY if there's a comment — silence is fine
Main + DT cross-pollination

Two accounts that feed each other. Main = brand + therapy + identity. DT = release valve + comedy + creative proof. Cross-traffic comes from specific tactical moments, not constant tagging.

When main account references DT

  • Slot 3 of Days 1, 4, 7 — DT teasers/reveals on main feed
  • When a sketch drops on DT — Slot 3 the same day on main = "the sketch is up"
  • Visible cardboard/glitter/uniform in MAIN videos as ambient cross-promo
  • Sugar bowl is on BOTH accounts — it's the connective tissue

When DT references main

  • NEVER promotional — DT stays in character mostly
  • Soft cross via comment section ("Captain has a side hustle on @rawhitmire" type)
  • Bio links to main — that's it
  • Process videos can be on either, depending on tone — "drawing the bridge" works as MAIN content if framed as identity / proof, OR as DT content if framed as "behind the show"
Cross-pollination cadence rule~30% of @rawhitmire content has visible DT elements. ~10% explicitly references DT account. DT account stays ~80% in-character with occasional process content.

Energy management · don't burn out before Cannes

Tuesday rest valve

No editing Tuesday eve. Slot 3 ships from Mon-prepped queue. If Tuesday rest fails 2 weeks running, queue-only the next day.

Wed batch sacred

10–14 main + 4 DT in one Wed shoot. Protect this day like a paid gig. If Wed wobbles, the rest of the week wobbles.

Sunday recovery

Sunday off-day. 8 AM wake. Light filming only (voiceovers, B-roll). 1-hour acting practice 5–6 PM. Sunday is NOT a make-up batch day.

Algorithm signals to optimize at each stage

Days 1-5 · Establish niche

  • Watch time — most important. 70% completion target.
  • Pillar consistency — TT will categorize you fast. Pillar 1 (Script Shift) every day teaches the algorithm what to surface you for.
  • Hashtag precision — 3 pillar tags + 2 evergreen, max 5. Don't dilute.
  • Comments engagement — reply to first 5 comments on every post within 60 min.

Days 6-11 · Compound + retain

  • Saves — strongest algorithmic signal. Every CTA includes "save if X."
  • Profile visits — bio + first 3 pinned videos do the work. Make sure pinned posts represent the brand spectrum.
  • Re-engagement — referencing prior posts ("the M04 hook" callback) trains audience that the account rewards rewatching.
  • Cross-post boost — Reels cross-post 30 min after TT to compound reach without algorithm penalty.

The strategy in one paragraph

9am = hook. 1pm = comments-bait. 5pm = release valve / DT crossover. Days 1-3 establish WHO. Days 4-7 prove WHAT. Days 8-10 raise WHY. Day 11 = Cannes. Hero posts max 2/wk. DT account builds in public for 4 days, drops first full sketch Day 5 as audience payoff. Tuesday rests, Wednesday batches, Sunday recovers.

Sugar bowl in every video. Voice over polish. Specific over vague. Comedy + emotional truth + identity rewrite + visible creative proof.
