Day 10 Decision Tree

Do this on Sat May 9 evening. Pull numbers from tracker. Calculate Format Scores. Identify top 3 winners. Weight remaining 21 days (Phases 2-3) accordingly. By Day 10, all 15 formats have ≥1 rep — see sprint plan for coverage.

📊 How to call the winners

  1. Calculate Format Score per format using:
    Score = (Avg Views × Avg Completion%) + (Comments × 50) + (Saves × 100) + (Shares × 75) + (Follows × 200)
    Why this weighting: completion% is the strongest algorithmic signal; saves, shares, and follows are higher-value than raw views; comments matter but are noisier.
  2. Rank all 15 formats by Score.
  3. Top 3 = winners. 70% of remaining slots (Days 11–31).
  4. Formats 4–6 = secondary. 20% of slots.
  5. Bottom 9 = drop or experiment. 10% reserved for new bold tests.
  6. Re-evaluate at Day 21. Sometimes a #4 ramps to #1 by Day 21. Don't lock too rigidly.
  7. Within 15% of each other = tied. Rotate equally.
  8. "Saves spike" formats: low views but very high saves often ramp later. Keep in rotation.
Quick worked example

Say after Day 10 you have these averages for F01 Direct Address (4 reps · M01 ⭐ · M07 ⭐ · M10 ⭐ · M19):

  • Avg views: 2,400
  • Avg completion: 28% (.28)
  • Avg comments: 8
  • Avg saves: 12
  • Avg shares: 5
  • Avg follows: 3

Score: (2400 × .28) + (8 × 50) + (12 × 100) + (5 × 75) + (3 × 200)

= 672 + 400 + 1200 + 375 + 600 = 3,247

Compare across all 15. The top 3 are your winners.

Day 21 mid-sprint check

Halfway through (May 20): if your top format has plateaued (no growth in views/follows over the last 5 reps), it's saturated for current audience size.

Three responses:

  1. Hold steady — that audience just isn't bigger yet. Keep posting, wait.
  2. Push secondary — invest hours into the next-best format to find your second hit.
  3. Try a bold experiment — location change, costume reveal, collab attempt. Sometimes a fresh angle resets the algorithm.

The sprint is a lab — don't get superstitious about a winning format that's stopped working.

After Day 31 — extending the lab

Post-sprint, drop to 2/day cadence and keep testing 1 new format/week. The winning format stays your bread + butter, but creators who stop testing get stale.

Treat the format library as a living document — when a new TT format emerges in 6 months, add it as F16, F17, etc., and run a 2-week test.

Find the format. Ride it. Then test the next one.
