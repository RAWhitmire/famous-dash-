Brand · vocabulary · in-house definitions

Lexicon.

Signature phrases, brand objects, and house terms — defined in one place. The voice is built out of these. Anyone landing on the brand for the first time should be able to read this and immediately get it.

"I did not audition for that role."

Signature phrase · most-used

The brand's load-bearing line. Used to refuse a version of yourself that other people cast you in — the reasonable one, the relieved one, the one with the stable plan. Always followed by "I am no longer available for the role of [X]."

e.g. "I am no longer available for the role of someone who waited until both versions of her agreed."

The working version

Posture · self-permission

The version of you that exists right now, with the chaos and the snacks and the hot glue. Distinct from the imagined version where you're "fine" or "ready." The working version is the only one actually doing the work.

The desk is the lab

Process · permission slip

Most of what you make doesn't leave the desk — and that's correct. The desk is where you find out what you keep returning to. The post is the byproduct, not the point.

Plan A funded by Plan B

Money · framing

The day-job (currently the print shop) is not the obstacle to the creative life. It's the runway that funds it. Refusing to clock in is what stalls the build. The day-job exits when the math says it can — not when frustration peaks.

Sarah

Audience · persona

The composite name for the audience this work is built for. 24–32, ND-leaning, identity-rewrite-coded. Performing inherited scripts. Creatively starved. Speaks more about what's not hers than what is. Sarah is who you're writing toward when you write — never the algorithm.

The sugar bowl

Visual · brand object

The recurring object in the corner of the frame. Functions as a visual anchor across videos so the room reads as the same room. Brand-deal-eligible at scale (candy / shelf-stable goods). Currently a placeholder until the actual sugar bowl is acquired.

The Polina note

Origin event · spine of the sprint

The Cannes selector telling me, publicly in a chat with 67 competitors visible, that my hook was generic. Three minutes to reply. Not the wound — the spec. The arc that runs through the sprint is built around honoring the note.

"Generic"

Diagnostic · use sparingly

Polina's word, now adopted. Definition: "could have come from anyone." If a line could come from any creator in your lane, it's not yours. Specificity is the moat.

PASSENGER

DM lead-magnet word · always all caps

The word viewers DM to receive the worksheet. Always all-caps in the pinned first comment. Lives in pinned first comments across the sprint. Never inside the in-video CTA voice (preserves the lead-magnet ritual).

"Make it so."

Discount Trek catchphrase

Closes Discount Trek videos only. Never on the main account. Borrowed from Picard, used unironically. The brand's permission-to-build phrase.

Discount Trek

Parallel project · @discounttrek

The cardboard Star Trek parody. Solo, drawn, glittered, fully unhinged. Only props are cardboard badges and phasers — no consoles, no large set pieces. The build itself is the show. First sketch dropped Day 10.

The 4-stack hook

Production · craft term

Every retention beat fires four signals at once: spoken VO, visual cue, on-screen text, and an audio cue or pattern interrupt. No silent cold opens. Every beat is its own hook, not just a body beat.

The working set

Site · meta

What this whole site is — the small set of references and rhythms worth keeping in one place. Anything that isn't in the working set doesn't earn its rent.

The voice is built from these. Anyone reading the lexicon should know what kind of work to expect.
