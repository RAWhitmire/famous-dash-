The Test Method read first

How This Works

  1. Days 1–10 (M01–M30): Cover all 15 formats, each posted at least 2x. Track views, completion %, comments-per-view, saves, shares, profile visits, follows.
  2. Day 10 evening: Calculate Format Score per format (formula in Decision Tree). Identify top 3 winners.
  3. Days 11–21 (M31–M63): 70% of slots go to top 3 formats (split by performance weight). 20% to next 3 promising formats. 10% to bold new tests (e.g., "what if I dressed up", "what if I shot in a weird location").
  4. Days 22–31 (M64–M93): 80% to confirmed winners, 10% maintenance of mid-tier, 10% bold experiments. By here you should have a signature format.
  5. Tracking happens in the table at the bottom of this page — fill in numbers daily, ranking auto-derives.
  6. The point: at 160 followers, your bottleneck is format-market fit, not effort. The fastest creators don't grind harder — they find what hits and stop wasting time on what doesn't.

Master Format Library 15 formats, 3 tiers

Tier A (gold) = high-virality formats, lower production cost. Tier B (teal) = medium-virality, builds brand depth. Tier C (purple) = low-virality but high-value (loyalty / SEO / repeat-watch).

F01

Direct Address

Face to camera, conversational. No costume, no setup. Punchy hook in first 1.5 sec. Strong opinion, story, or revelation. 15–45 sec.
When it worksPersonality is the product. You commenting on something hot. Anecdotes with a punchline. Hot takes.
Example"I just realized something nobody talks about with [thing]…"
F02

POV-Trope

"POV: you [scenario]" with you playing the audience experience. Visual + caption-driven, often no dialogue. 10–25 sec.
When it worksUniversal experiences. Niche subculture inside jokes. Recognizable scenarios. Looping endings.
Example"POV: you're a Trekkie at a normie party trying to act normal"
F03

Trend Remix

Hijack a trending sound or format. Apply YOUR twist. Original spin + recognizable framework = surface boost.
When it worksSound has 100K–10M uses, peak velocity, audience is YOUR audience. Find via TT search → trending → filter to last 7 days.
ExampleTrending "tell me you're X without telling me" sound + "tell me you're a solo creator without telling me"
F04

Storytime

Spoken anecdote, captions for retention. Strong opening hook ("the most unhinged thing happened..."). 30–60 sec. Builds parasocial bond.
When it worksTrue stories with a twist. Customer service nightmares. Filming mishaps. Anything self-deprecating.
Example"Storytime: I tried to film a Star Trek parody and the cardboard fell on me"
F05

Character Monologue (DT)

In Discount Trek character. Captain Discount addressing the camera/crew. 25–50 sec. Cardboard set + costume cue. Comedy + commitment to the bit.
When it worksThe bit is fully committed. Visible cardboard joins. Glitter usage. Direct breaks of the fourth wall WHILE in character.
ExampleCaptain Discount logs the captain's log about "the budget cuts that have impacted phaser quality"
F06

Voiceover B-Roll

Voiceover narration over visual content. You can record voice and footage separately = more polished. 25–60 sec.
When it worksBehind-the-scenes process. Building or making something. Information-dense content. Voice-first creators.
ExampleVoiceover explaining your sprint plan over footage of you setting up the cardboard bridge
F07

Mockumentary

Deadpan fake-documentary style. Cuts between B-roll and "interview" shots of yourself. Documentary Now! / Garth Marenghi energy. 30–60 sec.
When it worksSelf-deprecation packaged as serious. The bit benefits from production value mismatch. DT is a natural fit.
Example"This is the story of one woman, one room, and one cardboard bridge…"
F08

Process / Time-lapse

Time-lapse of you building, drawing, prepping. 15–40 sec. Speed-up music, satisfying reveal. Low dialogue requirement.
When it worksVisible progression. Final reveal is striking. Soothing pace. Great for sleep/study audiences who lurk + return.
ExampleDrawing the DT bridge in Photoshop, time-lapse + a reveal at the end
F09

Sketch / Mini-Scene

2+ characters (you in costume changes / ADR voices / split-screen). Narrative beat. 30–60 sec. Higher production but bigger payoff.
When it worksYou can clear costume changes in editing. Comedic beat works on rewatch. Punchline on the back end.
ExampleCaptain Discount + Salt Vampire arguing about budget through a split-screen "viewscreen"
F10

Reaction / Duet

React to or duet someone else's video. Borrows their reach. Strong commentary. 15–45 sec.
When it worksSource video is in your niche, recent, getting traction. You add genuine value (insight, joke, contradiction).
ExampleDuet a Trek take video adding the DT take. Or react to "things I learned in 6 years of acting" videos.
F11

Tutorial / How-To

Explain how you did something. Steps with text overlays. 30–60 sec. Saves > views (high algorithmic signal).
When it worksYou're teaching something only you can teach. "How to film a parody on no budget", "How to draw a Star Trek bridge in Photoshop in 30 min".
Example"How to make a cardboard ENTERPRISE bridge for under $20"
F12

Day-in-the-Life Mini-Vlog

Slice of life. Multiple cuts through your day. 30–60 sec. Higher production but builds parasocial bond hard.
When it worksYou're showing the LIFE behind the content (the print shop, the editing, the grind). Posts as parasocial fuel.
Example"Day in the life of a print shop employee who's secretly a content creator"
F13

Q&A / Audience Question

Answer a comment or DM on camera. Reply directly with text overlay. 15–40 sec. Direct community signal.
When it worksYou actually have audience questions (or fake one in early days). Reply-to-comment feature on TT amplifies these.
Example"Reply to @username asking how I have time to make all this content"
F14

Carousel / Photo Dump

Slideshow of photos with text overlays. Music underneath. 8–20 sec. TT loves these for completion rate.
When it worksVisual narrative. Before/after. Behind-the-scenes stills. Lookbook style. Easy to make on low-energy days.
Example"Things in my room that prove I'm becoming Discount Trek" — 8 photos with captions
F15

Green Screen Explainer

Chroma background, you commenting over visual. Often used for reactions to articles, posts, news. 20–45 sec.
When it worksYou need to point at / annotate something visual. News reactions. Article walkthroughs. Trek anniversary-related newsjacks.
ExampleGreen screen over the latest TOS 60th announcement, you commenting

Day 10 Decision Tree do this on May 9 evening

How to call the winners

  1. Calculate Format Score per format. Use this formula:
    Score = (Avg Views × Avg Completion%) + (Comments × 50) + (Saves × 100) + (Shares × 75) + (Follows × 200)
    Why this weighting: completion% is the strongest algorithmic signal; saves, shares, and follows are higher-value than raw views; comments matter but are noisier.
  2. Rank all 15 formats by Score.
  3. Top 3 formats = winners. These get 70% of remaining slots (Days 11–31).
  4. Formats 4–6 = secondary. 20% of slots — keep alive in case Format 1 plateaus.
  5. Bottom 9 formats = drop or experiment. 10% of slots reserved for new bold tests + occasional revisits.
  6. Re-evaluate at Day 21. Sometimes a format that ranked #4 on Day 10 ramps to #1 by Day 21 with continued reps. Don't lock too rigidly.
  7. If two formats are within 15% of each other on Format Score, treat as tied and rotate equally.
  8. Watch for "saves spike" formats. A format with low views but very high saves often ramps into the algorithm later — keep these in the rotation even if they look mid-tier on raw score.

What to do at Day 21 (May 20, halfway-ish): If your top format has plateaued (no growth in views/follows over the last 5 reps), it's saturated for current audience size. Either: (a) hold steady — that audience just isn't bigger yet, (b) push harder into the secondary set to find your next breakout, or (c) try a bold experiment (location change, costume reveal, collab attempt). The sprint is a lab — don't get superstitious about a winning format that's stopped working.
